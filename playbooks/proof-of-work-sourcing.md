*This playbook is in development. Publishing Q2 2026.*

## What will be in it

The complete Proof of Work Sourcing methodology, built from 18 months of testing across 28 client briefs. Four sections:

### Section 1 — Signal, not keyword

How to find ML engineers through the work they've shipped, not the profile they've written.

- **GitHub contributor graphs** — how to read activity patterns, distinguish signal from noise, identify active maintainers versus drive-by contributors
- **arXiv paper tracing** — tracking authors across papers, finding early-career researchers before they're on LinkedIn
- **HuggingFace model pages** — reading download patterns, understanding fine-tune lineage, finding engineers behind named models
- **Kaggle leaderboards** — what grandmaster status actually tells you about capability
- **Stack Overflow footprints** — identifying senior engineers through the questions they answer, not the questions they ask

### Section 2 — Reading their work fast

You don't have time to read every paper or audit every repo. Five techniques for extracting signal in under 90 seconds per candidate.

- The README-first pattern for GitHub repos
- The abstract-plus-conclusion method for arXiv papers
- Model card triage on HuggingFace
- How to evaluate code quality without reading the code
- When to spend 30 minutes instead of 2 minutes

### Section 3 — Outreach that converts

The specific patterns that got me 30-45% response rates on senior ML briefs.

- The four-sentence template (and why shorter fails)
- How to reference their work without sounding like an AI
- The specific-question close vs the "interested in chatting?" close
- Follow-up cadence for ML candidates (different from general tech)
- Common mistakes that immediately flag you as a bulk sender

### Section 4 — The full scripts

Real outreach messages from real briefs, with response rates. Edited to remove candidate details, but otherwise verbatim.

- 12 outreach messages for specific brief types (MLE, Research Scientist, ML Platform, ML Ops)
- The 5 follow-up templates I use
- The brief-to-outreach translation pattern

## Why it's taking this long

Every technique in here has been tested on live briefs. Every response rate number is pulled from my actual CRM data. Every outreach script has been iterated 10+ times. The playbook will ship when the methodology is tight enough that a recruiter reading it can hit 30%+ response rates in their first week using it.
